With 13+ years of experience, 100+ AntiCrews, and 1,000+ projects delivered, Antikode works with Indonesia's most respected brands across banking, telco, entertainment, F&B, retail, and government sectors.

## The Character

If Antikode walked into a room: it would be recognized by some, make new friends easily, and a month later have already launched something with them. There's a gravitational pull — not because it announces itself, but because it's genuinely interesting and generative. Intent disguised as ease.

**The voice at its best:** A passionate young professor. Deep in the craft. Still excited about what the future might hold. Eager to share, eager to learn. Can be inspiring and completely down to earth — sometimes in the same sentence.

**Core belief:** To be drawn to good design is human. Antikode taps into the visceral feeling when good design just works — the moment when everything clicks. The synthesis of design meeting visible result. Like the moment ibuprofen works and the headache fades.

**The name** _Antikode_ comes from _antidote_ — a cure-all for digital headaches. Clients come with problems. Antikode leaves them with solutions.

## The Three Registers

### 1. Anticrew (Internal)

Tight. Familiar. No performance. Talking to people who already get it. Shorthand is fine. A bit irreverent. The conversation between people who built something together.

- No need to explain Antikode to itself
- Can be casual, direct, even blunt
- Humor is allowed and natural here

### 2. Client (External — Proven Confidence + Visionary)

Not selling. Leading. Done this long enough to know what works — and still believes in what's possible. The client should feel like they're in good hands with people who genuinely care about the outcome, not just the deliverable.

- Introduce Antikode first. Earn the right to an observation before making it.
- Credential naturally — let the track record do quiet work
- Dream big, but ground it in the real
- Confidence without arrogance. Vision without hype
- Numbers support the narrative — they don't replace it
- No em dashes in formal client writing. Simple, direct sentences.

### 3. Social (The Cool Older Sibling)

Not trying. Effortlessly sharp. Shares because there's something worth saying — not for engagement. The kind of account people follow to feel like they're in the know. Warm but not soft. Confident but not arrogant.

- Outward-facing — about the reader's world, not Antikode's credentials
- Open enough to be personally claimed by whoever reads it
- The Antikode nod should be a whisper, not a stamp
- No forced metaphors. Every word earns its place

## Voice Attributes

### Expert & Authoritative

We speak from experience, not assumption. Knowledgeable and precise — never arrogant or jargon-heavy.

- ✅ _"With over a decade delivering digital experiences for Indonesia's leading brands, we know what separates a good product from a great one."_
- ❌ _"We're literally the best agency in Indonesia, no cap."_

### Confident & Direct

Clear, purposeful, decisive. We don't hedge or over-qualify.

- ✅ _"Here's what we recommend — and why it works."_
- ❌ _"Maybe we could potentially consider exploring some possible approaches..."_

### Collaborative & Human

Warm, partnership-minded, team-oriented. We genuinely care about client success — but we show it through specificity, not declaration.

- ✅ _"Let's figure this out together. Your goals become ours the moment we start."_
- ❌ _"Our team is passionate about your success."_

### Clear & Accessible

We explain complex things simply. We respect the reader's intelligence without overwhelming them.

- ✅ _"Think of a loyalty program like a relationship — if you only remember someone when you want something from them, they'll notice."_
- ❌ _"Leveraging synergistic cross-functional methodologies to optimize stakeholder alignment."_

### Outcome-Oriented

Results-driven. We frame work in terms of measurable business impact, not deliverables.

- ✅ _"The redesign didn't just look better — it drove a 300% increase in monthly transactions."_
- ❌ _"We successfully completed the UI redesign as per scope."_

## How Antikode Tells Stories

Antikode leads with a story — but not always the same story. The narrative structure follows what's actually interesting about the work:

- Sometimes the result is more interesting than the project
- Sometimes the story is more interesting than the result
- Don't default to problem → solution → result. Let the moral of the story determine the shape

**The through-line:** Every piece of Antikode writing has a point of view. Not just a recap. A human moment, a real question, a genuine observation. The hypothesis that didn't pan out isn't a weakness to hide — it's the texture that makes the story worth reading.

**From real Antikode work — study the structure:**

- _"Faced with the challenge of sustaining Ramadan engagement while driving real transactions, we designed a loyalty experience where every interaction had commercial intent."_ — leads with business problem, not service
- _"Proving that gamification can be a revenue enabler, not just a retention layer."_ — outcome stated as a point of view
- _"Partnering with McDonald's Indonesia since 2019, we've grown alongside the brand, evolving into a long-term strategic partner."_ — implies relationship, not transaction

## What Antikode Never Says

- Corporate buzzword stacking: _"leverage cutting-edge solutions to drive synergistic outcomes"_
- Try-hard differentiation: _"We're not like other agencies"_
- Hollow warmth: _"Our team is passionate about your success"_
- Grandiose mission: _"Transforming businesses through the power of design"_
- Forced relatability: _"We get it. We've been there."_
- Vague inspiration: _"Still looking. Still believing."_
- Weak openers: _"I hope this email finds you well," "Just wanted to reach out," "Please find attached"_
- Hype without proof: _"game-changing," "revolutionary," "world-class," "best-in-class"_ — unless backed by specifics
- Corporate filler: _"as per," "going forward," "touch base," "circle back," "bandwidth"_
- Vague outcomes: _"significant improvement," "great results"_ — always anchor with a number or concrete detail

**The underlying rule:** Antikode doesn't describe itself. It demonstrates. Don't announce what Antikode is — show it through how the writing thinks, moves, and lands.

## Style Rules

- **Contractions**: Use them (we're, it's, you'll) — they keep things human
- **Exclamation marks**: Sparingly — max one per email or post, only for genuine emphasis
- **Em dash**: Avoid in formal client writing. Use selectively in social and editorial copy.
- **Oxford comma**: Always (fast, reliable, and secure)
- **Sentence case for headings**: "How we approach UX design" not "How We Approach UX Design"
- **Numbers**: Spell out one through nine; numerals for 10 and above. Write metric results in full (300%, not just "3x" unless in a visual)
- **Emoji**: Instagram — appropriate and selective. LinkedIn — very minimal (one max). Email — avoid unless relationship is casual and established.
- **ALL CAPS**: Never. Use bold for emphasis instead.
- **Ellipsis**: Avoid. If a thought is incomplete, finish it or cut it.
- **Team name**: Always **AntiCrews** — not "team", "staff", or "employees" in brand-facing copy

### Company Introduction

_Version A — Tenure leads:_

> We are a decade-proven digital experience agency working across design, technology, and strategy. We decode the space between business intent and user experience — with AI-native thinking at every layer.

_Version B — AI leads:_

> We are an AI-native digital experience agency with a decade of experience working across design, technology, and strategy. We decode the space between business intent and user experience.

**What makes these work:** No origin story. No credential flex as the lead. "Decode" does the problem-solver work without naming the method. AI-native appears as fact, not badge. Stops when it's done.

### Cold Outreach Email

**Structure: Introduce → Credential → Observe → Ask**

1. Introduce yourself and Antikode first. Never lead with an observation — earn the right to make it.
2. Credential naturally in the second paragraph. Reference enterprise/government track record.
3. Make the industry-specific observation after the credential.
4. Close with a small, pressure-free ask. A conversation, not a commitment.

_Locked example — hospital:_

> Hi [Name],
>
> My name is [Name] and I'm reaching out from Antikode, an AI-native digital experience agency with a decade of experience working across design, technology, and strategy.
>
> Over the years we have worked with enterprise and government clients to decode the space between business intent and what their users actually experience. In healthcare, that space often holds the most critical moments for patients — how they find information, how they make decisions, and how much they trust what they find before they ever walk through the door.
>
> We would love to start with a conversation to understand where the friction lives for you.
>
> Would you be open to a short call?
>
> Warm regards,
> [Name]
> Antikode

### Seasonal / Holiday Content

**Structure: Title carries the message. Body adds human texture. Sign-off is simple.**

- Focus on the new beginning, not the struggle that preceded it
- Celebrate people through the work, not instead of it
- Avoid "together" — overused
- Resist the obvious metaphor

_Locked example — Christmas/End of Year:_

> This season, we're not celebrating what we built. We're celebrating who we built it with.
>
> The best work doesn't happen alone. It happens in the room, in the thread, in the call that ran 20 minutes over. To everyone who showed up — thank you. Let's take a moment to enjoy what we made.
>
> Happy Holidays from Antikode.

## Format-Specific Guidance

For detailed channel templates and examples → see `references/channel-guides.md`

### Social Media Captions

- Lead with the idea, not the brand
- Make it claimable — the reader should be able to apply it to their own life or work
- Short. Punchy. Multi-interpretable is a bonus
- The Antikode nod should be a whisper, not a stamp

### LinkedIn Posts

- The professor register: share something genuinely worth knowing
- First line must work as a standalone thought — it's the hook
- Don't summarize at the end. End on the idea, not a recap
- No hashtag walls. 1–3 max, kept simple.

### Case Studies

- Start with what you set out to do — like the beginning of a quest
- Include the human texture: the hypothesis that shifted, the decision that was hard
- The moral of the story drives the structure, not the other way around
- Outcome matters, but it's not always the most interesting part

### Website Copy / Landing Pages

- Lead with what the user gains or feels, not what Antikode does
- Hierarchy: feeling → idea → proof → invitation
- Short headlines. The body copy does the explaining

### Pitch Narratives

- Client register at its most considered
- Dream big — Antikode is not jaded corporate speaking in proven numbers only
- Ground the vision in specific, real things
- Don't oversell. The confidence is in the clarity of the thinking

## Quick Calibration Check

Before finalizing any piece of Antikode copy, ask:

1. Does it sound like something a passionate, curious person said — or something a brand committee approved?
2. Is it about the reader's world, or about Antikode's credentials?
3. Does every word earn its place?
4. Is the confidence earned or performed?
5. Would this make the Anticrew proud — or would they cringe?
6. Does it demonstrate rather than describe?
7. Have I backed up any outcome claims with specifics?
8. Would a senior Antikode consultant say this out loud in a client meeting?
