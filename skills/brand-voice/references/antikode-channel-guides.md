## Email to Client

### Structure
1. **Opening** — Context or relationship anchor. No "I hope this finds you well."
2. **Body** — The point. One clear message per email.
3. **Close** — Specific next step or ask

### Good Opening Examples
- "Following our call last Thursday, here's what we've put together."
- "Quick update on the [Project Name] build — we hit a milestone worth sharing."
- "We've been looking at your brief and want to share an early perspective."

### Bad Opening Examples (avoid)
- "I hope this email finds you well."
- "Just wanted to touch base on..."
- "Please find attached the document as discussed."

### Cold Outreach Structure: Introduce → Credential → Observe → Ask
- Never lead with an observation. Earn the right to make it first.
- Introduce Antikode and yourself in the opening paragraph.
- Credential in the second paragraph — reference enterprise/government track record quietly.
- Make the industry-specific observation after the credential.
- Close with a pressure-free ask. A conversation, not a pitch.
- No em dashes. Simple, direct sentences.

### Sample: Project Update Email
---
Subject: [Project Name] — Sprint 2 Update

Hi [Name],

We wrapped Sprint 2 this week and are on track for the [milestone] delivery on [date].

A few things worth noting:
- [Completed item] — live and tested
- [In progress] — on schedule, no blockers
- [Risk or flag] — we're monitoring this closely and will confirm by [date]

Next step from our side: [X]. From yours, we'll need [Y] by [date].

Let us know if you'd like to walk through anything in more detail.

[Signature]

## Proposal / Deck Copy

### Principles
- Open with insight about the client's world, not with Antikode's credentials
- Use specifics over generalities ("reduced checkout drop-off by 30%" beats "improved user experience")
- Structure: Problem → Approach → Proof → Team → Next Step
- Keep section headers declarative: "What we'll build" not "Our Proposed Solution"

### Good Proposal Opener
"Indonesia's digital consumers expect experiences that are fast, intuitive, and personal. [Client] has the brand — we'll build the experience that matches it."

### Bad Proposal Opener (avoid)
"Antikode is a leading digital agency with over 10 years of experience delivering world-class solutions across diverse industries."

## LinkedIn Post

### Format
- 3–5 short paragraphs, no bullet overload
- First line must earn the scroll — no weak openers
- One clear idea per post
- Optional: 1–3 relevant hashtags at the end, max
- Avoid tagging people unnecessarily

### Good First Lines
- "Most digital projects don't fail because of bad code. They fail because of unclear requirements."
- "We just wrapped something we're genuinely proud of."
- "Here's what 13 years of building digital products in Indonesia has taught us."

### Bad First Lines (avoid)
- "Excited to share that..."
- "We are pleased to announce..."
- "In today's fast-paced digital landscape..."

### Sample Post Structure
---
[Hook — one punchy sentence or observation]

[Context — 2–3 sentences explaining the situation or insight]

[The point — what this means, what we learned, or what we did]

[Optional close — invitation to engage or reflect]

#DigitalExperience #UXDesign #Antikode

## Instagram Caption

### How Antikode Writes on Instagram
- **Short** — rarely more than 3–4 sentences. The visual does the heavy lifting.
- **Confident but not loud** — no exclamation spam, no hype
- **Purposeful** — every post has a clear category: project showcase, insight, collab, or celebration

### Content Types & Patterns

**Project Showcase** — lead with brand/campaign concept, not process
> "Active. Dynamic. Made to move. A new digital hub for POCARI SWEAT that blends storytelling and interactive experiences through a modern design."
- Hashtag: `#byAntikode`
- Formula: [3-word brand concept]. [1–2 sentence description of what was built and why].

**Insight / Thought Leadership** — provocative visual headline + minimal caption
> Caption: "Skip the blind revamp. Our SEO Specialist shares 4 steps to do it right. Get the guide at link in bio."
- Hashtag: `#AntikodeInsight`
- Credit the individual AntiCrew by name and title on the visual
- Caption is a sharp 2-liner — the visual carries the argument

**Collaboration / Industry** — warm, understated, genuine
> "Looking back at the UI/UX Design Incubator Program with Purwadhika School of Design. Thanks for having us contribute. Hope this program becomes a useful step toward new perspectives and stronger skills."
- No hype. Short. Acknowledges the partner, not Antikode's achievement.

**Holiday / Celebration** — the one place Antikode gets fully warm and playful
- Emoji allowed more freely here (2–3 is fine)
- Casual, warm, human
- Focus on people and the future, not struggle or brokenness
- Resist the obvious metaphor (don't make Easter about eggs)

### Format Rules
- Caption: 2–4 sentences max
- Hashtags: 1–3, kept simple (`#byAntikode`, `#AntikodeInsight`)
- Emoji: 1–2 for regular posts; 2–3 allowed for holidays/celebrations
- No hashtag walls
- Credit individuals by name + role on the visual
- For award nominations: use "humbled" framing, not boastful

### What to Avoid on Instagram
- Caption longer than 4 sentences
- Hashtag spam
- Excessive exclamation points
- Describing the process instead of the outcome or concept

## Blog / Insights Article

### Structure
- **Title**: Specific and useful. "How UX Designers and System Analysts Work Together" beats "The Power of Collaboration"
- **Opening**: A concrete scenario, observation, or question — not a definition
- **Body**: Use analogies to explain complex concepts; break with subheadings every 200–300 words
- **Conclusion**: A clear takeaway the reader can act on or remember

### Tone Notes
- Write like you're explaining to a smart colleague outside your discipline
- It's OK to have a point of view — bland "on one hand / on the other hand" pieces don't build authority
- Cite real examples from Antikode's work where possible (anonymize if needed)
- Credit the author by name and role

### Good Article Opener
"There's no magic wand involved in crafting user-friendly design. At Antikode, real magic happens through collaboration — not illusions."

### Bad Article Opener (avoid)
"In today's rapidly evolving digital landscape, organizations are increasingly recognizing the importance of user experience."

## Seasonal & Greeting Content

### Principles
- Title carries the full message on its own
- Body adds human texture — specific, not generic
- Sign-off is simple and warm
- Focus on possibility and people, not struggle or brokenness
- Outward-facing — about the reader's world, not Antikode's journey

### Locked Examples

**Christmas / End of Year:**
> This season, we're not celebrating what we built. We're celebrating who we built it with.
>
> The best work doesn't happen alone. It happens in the room, in the thread, in the call that ran 20 minutes over. To everyone who showed up — thank you. Let's take a moment to enjoy what we made.
>
> Happy Holidays from Antikode.

**Easter (rebirth and new beginnings):**
- ✅ The Future Is Worth Building
- ✅ What we build today blooms tomorrow
- ✅ Designed to rise
- ❌ Even broken things bloom again
- ❌ Hope keeps the shell warm

**Valentine's (love comes in many forms):**
- Love Doesn't Have One Face
- Every Kind Counts
- More Ways to Love Than We Were Taught
- It Looks Different on Everyone
- All of It Is Real
